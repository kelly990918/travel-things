Taxi

...We always advice tourists to take Ubers in the city since some drivers would scam passengers. If you're going from Hong Kong Island to Kowloon or New Territories? That's too far away and cross-harbour tunnels are expensive, get off! If the trip is less than 5 minutes? That's too short, just walk, get off!

So, what's my advice? Take an Uber~

The only taxi you can trust are those from the airport. When you take those taxis a staffer will give you a ticket on the price range from the airport to your destination, so the taxi driver can't play tricks.

Kelly's Observations

Education

The education system kind of follows the British system‚Äî3 years kindergarten + 6 years primary + 6 years secondary (junior high + high school) + 4 years university. Primary and secondary education are complimentary for public schools.

Public school teachers are counted as civil servants, enjoying stable and high income (the starting salary is ~$3960USD/ month). It's a popular role for university graduates because of the stability of the job, not all of the them are passionate teachers.

Many schools in Hong Kong were founded by missionaries, and most of them are girls/ boys schools. These church schools made up most of the top schools in the city, so most of the top students were grow up in the strict and oppressive environment. Even though they may excel academically, some students have very weak social skills and even develop fear to interact with the opposite sex.

Social Media

Hong Kong is not included in the Chinese wall, so we can access Meta and Google. The most popular platforms are Threads, Instagram

You might ask, what about TikTok/ Douyin? Well, Douyin was popular since I was in high school a decade ago. At that time, it's already famous for dumb videos, and because it went popular around the 2014 protests when people called to boycott Chinese products, local people at my age don't use that app. But I've seen millenials using it. It's just not for my generation.
